# Realtime Execution Evidence Protocol V0

Date: 2026-07-15

Status: approved on 2026-07-15. Primitive reporting, chamber capture, local
durable custody, and explicit local/remote distribution identity are
implemented through Sprint 6D.4. The immutable ledger and processing-authority
foundation are implemented in Sprint 6D.5A. Trusted providers, processor
activation, and acknowledged-prefix compaction remain Sprint 6D.5B through
6D.5D.

Coherence review:
[`coherence-review-v0.md`](./coherence-review-v0.md).

## Purpose

This protocol defines one coherent execution truth for Cadenza business logic
across primitive execution, local graph runs, chamber boundaries, cell-to-cell
distribution, detached signals, and child inquiries.

Execution evidence is not ordinary logging, metrics, or result persistence. It
is the structured account of what consequential work occurred, how it was
related, under which authority it ran, and where trustworthy custody of that
account began.

The protocol serves the intended whole by absorbing observability and
distributed-correlation complexity below authored business logic. A graph
architect must not add tracing tasks, propagate infrastructure identifiers, or
design deployment-aware flows merely to make ordinary execution interpretable.

## Design Sources

The protocol keeps useful lessons from the legacy `cadenza-service` and
`cadenza-db` implementations without treating their APIs, schemas, naming, or
persistence model as authority:

- detached signals began new local graph runs while preserving an overarching
  execution trace.
- traces, graph runs, task executions, signal emissions, and inquiries had
  distinct identities.
- remote calls carried causal identities across service boundaries.
- evidence processing used Cadenza meta graphs.
- database ingestion reconciled dependent records that arrived out of order.
- production suppressed recursive meta detail while debugging could expose it.

The new protocol repairs the legacy dependence on raw context persistence,
mutable execution rows as evidence authority, asynchronous meta signals as the
first capture boundary, inferred field normalization, and service location as
an incomplete representation of distribution.

## Authority

The language-neutral semantic contract belongs to `cadenza` because it
defines the meaning of primitive and graph execution across all core language
expressions.

Ownership remains layered:

- `cadenza` owns trace, graph-execution, primitive-execution, relationship,
  inquiry, and context-composition evidence semantics.
- `cadenza-chamber` owns trustworthy adapter and chamber capture, local
  execution-source sequencing, materialization links, and normalized runtime
  outcomes.
- `cadenza-cell` owns local custody, cell-generation binding, distribution-call
  and transport-attempt capture, and evidence publication boundaries.
- `cadenza-environment` owns durable custody/ledger contracts, policy-driven
  processing, durable projection, replay, compaction, streaming, aggregation,
  and authorized disclosure. Its persistence adapters do not redefine
  execution meaning.

This authority assignment is recorded in `contracts.config.json`.

## Trust Model

The protocol assumes:

- database-authored callable source and language adapters may be faulty or
  hostile.
- chamber and cell processes can fail independently.
- transport can duplicate, delay, reorder, or lose delivery.
- wall clocks can disagree.
- business context can contain secrets or regulated data.
- downstream evidence processors and databases can be unavailable.
- evidence-processing logic can itself fail or recurse.

An authored task or language adapter may contribute diagnostic detail, but it
must not be the sole authority that its own execution occurred. Authoritative
capture belongs to the chamber or cell boundary that observes and controls the
transition.

## Execution Identities

### Trace

`trace_key` identifies the complete causal whole initiated by one root intent,
inquiry, signal, or directly invoked graph.

A trace:

- survives graph boundaries, detached signals, inquiries, delegations,
  chambers, cells, and language runtimes.
- does not imply request/response, parent waiting, or result ownership.
- is a causal identity rather than a conventional timed span.
- has one root event but does not require a V0 closing event.

V0 does not claim that global trace quiescence can be known without a future
trace steward. Individual graph executions and distribution calls do have
authoritative conclusions.

### Trace Creation And Propagation

The first trusted ingress creates `trace_key` when no valid internal causal
trace exists. Authored business context, external request headers, callable
return values, and language-native tracing libraries cannot create, replace, or
override the authoritative key.

An existing trace may continue only through trusted runtime metadata or an
authenticated internal envelope that binds the key. A chamber or cell rejects
conflicting trace identities rather than choosing one. External trace headers
may be retained as separately named correlation commitments, but they are not
Cadenza trace authority.

Trace, graph, inquiry, distribution, transport-attempt, and evidence identities
must remain outside user context in every official core language.

### Graph Execution

`graph_execution_key` identifies one local run of one graph in one chamber.

It binds at least:

- graph definition identity and version.
- chamber, image, adapter, and cell-generation identities.
- trace identity.
- ingress cause.
- immutable run-baseline context commitment.
- conclusion outcome and resulting-context commitment when available.

A detached signal starts a new graph execution. A local or distributed inquiry
responder starts a new graph execution. Neither action starts a new trace when
it already has a valid causal trace.

### Primitive And Effect Execution

The following identities are distinct even when one causes another:

- `task_execution_key`: one attempt to execute one task definition.
- `signal_emission_key`: one emission of one signal.
- `inquiry_key`: one logical request/response call created by an intent inquiry.
- `inquiry_responder_key`: one responder obligation within an inquiry call.
- `relationship_execution_key`: one runtime resolution of an authored task
  relationship.
- `composition_key`: one graph-conclusion composition attempt.

Retries create new task-execution identities unless an approved primitive
contract explicitly defines a resumed attempt. Definitions and executions must
never share an identity.

### Distribution Execution

`distribution_execution_key` identifies one logical cell-to-cell operation.
It connects the source primitive effect to the target ingress and, for
delegation, the target graph conclusion back to the source outcome.

`transport_attempt_key` identifies one concrete attempt to carry that logical
operation. Retry, reconnect, or future rerouting creates a new attempt while
preserving the distribution-execution identity.

V0 direct transport normally has one attempt, but the distinction prevents
future retries from being misrepresented as unrelated business calls.

### Evidence Record

`evidence_key` identifies one immutable observation. Re-delivery of the same
record preserves its key and digest. A correction or later state transition is
a new record that causally references the earlier record; evidence is never
rewritten.

## Identity Invariants

1. Detachment ends a response obligation, not trace membership.
2. Every graph execution belongs to exactly one trace.
3. Every task execution belongs to exactly one graph execution.
4. Every signal emission has exactly one source graph execution when emitted
   from a graph; a root external signal names an external ingress cause. One
   emission may cause zero or more target graph executions.
5. An inquiry keeps one `inquiry_key` across its requester and all responder
   graph executions. Each selected responder has one
   `inquiry_responder_key`, and the inquiry conclusion composes their outcomes.
6. Each responder graph execution belongs to exactly one inquiry responder.
7. A distributed operation keeps one `distribution_execution_key` across
   source, transport attempts, target ingress, and source outcome.
8. Each transport attempt belongs to exactly one distribution execution.
9. Cross-source causality is established by keys and causal references, never
   inferred from timestamps or arrival order.
10. Source-local sequence numbers order records from one capture source only.
11. Chamber and transport contracts carry these identities explicitly. A
    generic correlation key cannot substitute for any execution identity.

## Required Causal Flows

### Detached Signal

```text
trace T
  graph execution G1
    task execution A
      signal emission S
  graph execution G2 caused_by S
  graph execution G3 caused_by S
```

Every caused graph keeps `G1.trace_key`. None has a response obligation to
`G1`. Signal acceptance means bounded ingress was accepted; it does not mean
any caused graph completed. Zero observers is also a valid evidenced outcome.

### Local Inquiry

```text
trace T
  graph execution G1
    task execution A
      inquiry I
        responder R1 -> graph execution G2 -> conclusion C1
        responder R2 -> graph execution G3 -> conclusion C2
  inquiry I concluded_by composition(C1, C2)
```

An inquiry may select one or more responders. Each gets a distinct responder
identity and graph execution. The inquiry result returns through `I` after its
responder outcomes are composed; graph trace membership alone does not create a
result relationship.

### Distributed Delegation

```text
trace T
  source graph G1 / task A
    distribution execution D
      transport attempt P1
      target graph G2
        graph conclusion C
    source outcome caused_by C
```

Transport phases and graph phases remain separately interpretable. A transport
failure must preserve whether target execution was known to have started.

### Distributed Detached Signal

```text
trace T
  source graph G1 / signal emission S
    distribution execution D
      transport attempt P1
      target signal ingress caused_by S and D
      target graph G2 caused_by S
```

The source receives an acceptance or failure outcome for delivery, never the
business result of `G2`.

## Evidence Event Envelope

Every record must use a bounded, versioned, canonical envelope equivalent to:

```json
{
  "contract": "cadenza.execution-evidence",
  "version": "0",
  "evidence_key": "evidence.execution.example",
  "event_type": "task.completed",
  "observation_basis": "runtime_reported",
  "reporting_source": {
    "runtime_language": "typescript",
    "runtime_image_digest": "sha256:...",
    "adapter_key": "adapter.typescript"
  },
  "observed_at_unix_ms": 0,
  "capture_source": {
    "environment_key": "environment.example",
    "cell_key": "cell.example",
    "cell_generation_key": "cell-generation.example",
    "chamber_key": "chamber.example",
    "chamber_image_epoch": 1,
    "source_sequence": 42
  },
  "execution": {
    "trace_key": "trace.example",
    "graph_execution_key": "graph-execution.example",
    "task_execution_key": "task-execution.example"
  },
  "causes": [
    {
      "kind": "relationship_execution",
      "key": "relationship-execution.example"
    }
  ],
  "authority": {
    "definition_key": "task.example",
    "definition_version_key": "task-version.example",
    "authority_revision": 7,
    "policy_decision_key": "decision.example"
  },
  "outcome": "completed",
  "commitments": {
    "input_context_digest": "sha256:...",
    "result_context_digest": "sha256:..."
  },
  "previous_source_evidence_digest": "sha256:...",
  "evidence_digest": "sha256:..."
}
```

The exact field optionality and canonical fixtures belong to implementation
design. V0 semantic requirements are:

- contract and version.
- globally unique evidence identity.
- typed event and normalized outcome.
- observation basis that distinguishes `runtime_reported`,
  `chamber_observed`, `cell_observed`, and `authority_committed` evidence.
- reporting-source identity for runtime-reported evidence.
- observation time for operations, not causal ordering.
- trustworthy capture-source identity and source-local sequence.
- all applicable execution identities.
- explicit causal references.
- applicable definition, authority, policy, image, and adapter references.
- context or artifact commitments rather than raw sensitive values.
- a source-local digest chain or equivalent tamper-evident ordering mechanism.
- deterministic evidence digest.

Local custody finalizes the source sequence and digest linkage and returns a
custody receipt. Exported records or bounded batches must carry a cell-generation
attestation over their digest or chain root so a durable ledger can verify
capture provenance after transport. The exact receipt and batch-attestation
shape is an implementation design gate.

Unknown fields fail validation at trusted capture boundaries. Evolution uses a
new contract version rather than permissive shape drift.

The observation basis states what was actually known:

- `runtime_reported`: a digest-bound approved runtime reported an internal
  semantic event, and trusted substrate preserved that report.
- `chamber_observed`: the chamber directly controlled or observed the runtime
  boundary.
- `cell_observed`: the cell directly controlled or observed custody, routing,
  containment, or distribution.
- `authority_committed`: a trusted provider created the evidence atomically
  with an authority or ledger transaction.

Runtime-reported task evidence is not represented as independent chamber proof
of language-runtime internals. External capability and distribution affect
requires a direct chamber, cell, or authority observation in addition to any
runtime report.

## Required Event Families

V0 must be able to represent at least:

- `trace.started`.
- `graph.accepted`, `graph.started`, and `graph.concluded`.
- `task.scheduled`, `task.started`, `task.completed`, and `task.failed`.
- `relationship.resolved` with traversal, non-traversal, and contribution
  policy outcome.
- `signal.emitted`, `signal.ingress_accepted`, and
  `signal.ingress_rejected`.
- `inquiry.started`, `inquiry.completed`, and `inquiry.failed`.
- `inquiry.responder_started`, `inquiry.responder_completed`, and
  `inquiry.responder_failed`.
- `composition.completed` and `composition.failed`.
- `distribution.requested`, `distribution.accepted`,
  `distribution.started`, and `distribution.outcome`.
- `transport.attempted`, `transport.accepted`, `transport.started`, and
  `transport.outcome`.
- `evidence_batch.accepted`, `evidence_batch.completed`, and
  `evidence_batch.failed` for evidence-processing boundaries.

Not every successful transition requires a separate persisted row. The event
families define semantic facts; batching and projections may compact their
storage without discarding the authoritative append history.

Progress updates, stdout, metrics, and ordinary logs are diagnostics. They are
not mandatory evidence unless an authority policy promotes a specific bounded
fact into the protocol.

## Context Commitments And Disclosure

The default envelope must never contain raw business input, result context,
secret value, credential, source text, certificate, key, endpoint, stdout, or
stack trace.

Structural evidence may contain:

- canonical whole-context digests.
- bounded changed-path commitments when policy permits field-name disclosure.
- schema identity and validation outcome.
- output size and normalized failure class.
- conflict paths and terminal provenance allowed by the graph-conclusion
  contract.
- opaque subject and runtime-principal references or commitments when required
  for accountability.

Authorized diagnostic material is a separate disclosure artifact with its own
identity, encryption, access policy, retention, size bound, and evidence link.
Enabling debug evidence does not silently place raw context inside the base
evidence stream.

Canonicalization and digest algorithms must be identical across official core
languages and protected by shared fixtures before implementation closes.

## Custody Boundary

For an affect-bearing transition, the minimum required evidence record must
enter durable bounded local custody before the transition is reported or
committed as successful.

The sequence is:

1. the trusted observer constructs and validates the minimum record.
2. the local custody sink assigns or validates source sequence and digest
   linkage.
3. the sink acknowledges durable bounded custody.
4. the controlled transition may commit or report success.
5. downstream processing, streaming, and durable projection proceed
   asynchronously.

Failure to accept mandatory evidence fails the transition closed. Failure of a
later database or projection does not retroactively invalidate work whose
evidence is already in trustworthy local custody.

Runtime queue acceptance alone is not custody. Official runtime reporters must
support an abstract barrier through a submitted report sequence. The barrier
resolves only after the cell has durably accepted all mandatory evidence through
that sequence. Core runtimes remain persistence-agnostic because they know the
receipt contract, not the journal or provider that implements it.

An external capability or distribution effect requires custody both before the
effect is performed and after its outcome is observed but before success or safe
failure is returned. If affect may have occurred and terminal custody fails,
the outcome is `evidence_integrity_failed`, not safely retryable.

Fail-closed has different meanings before and after execution starts:

- before callable invocation or external affect, missing custody prevents the
  transition.
- after execution has started, failure to capture a required terminal record
  cannot erase possible affect. The runtime reports an
  `evidence_integrity_failed` outcome, must not claim safe retry, and quarantines
  or drains the affected capture source until custody is repaired.
- recovery appends an explicit sequence-gap or unrecoverable-gap record; it
  never fabricates the missing business outcome.

Diagnostic-only records may be sampled, aggregated, or dropped under explicit
policy. Mandatory structural or boundary records may not.

The V0 local journal is a cell-generation-owned append-only segmented journal
opened through a private directory descriptor. It assigns a custody sequence,
preserves source and custody digest chains, synchronizes accepted records before
returning `cell_durable`, and seals immutable evidence-key-signed batches.

Normal high-water stops new execution admission before affect. A separate
bounded terminal reserve is available only for closing work already admitted.
Segment count, bytes, age, unacknowledged backlog, total resident bytes, and
processor claim leases are bounded. Evidence is never overwritten to recover
capacity.

The evidence processor claims the oldest unacknowledged batch and acknowledges
it only with a canonical receipt binding the batch key, root digest, durable
ledger commit identity, and committed time. Sprint 6D.4 retains acknowledged
signed segments because they remain the local authority for recovery and equal
replay. Physical deletion and compaction require Sprint 6D.5 ledger authority
for historical sequence, digest, and replay resolution. An in-memory queue
alone is not durable custody.

Sprint 6D.5A establishes the isolated immutable PostgreSQL ledger, restricted
append role, canonical-byte verification, signed custody-entry commitments,
claim-scoped processing-attempt records, atomic commit receipts, terminal
`authority_committed` completion boundaries, and rebuildable regular views.
Equal-root replay must reproduce the committed manifest and custody bytes
exactly. A retry may append only a complete digest-chained processing-attempt
suffix before the original receipt is returned, so unknown-commit evidence is
not discarded when local acknowledgement clears its sidecar.
The database does not materialize or invoke the processor. Manifest signature
verification, typed custody and ledger providers, and exact receipt
acknowledgement remain trusted cell responsibilities introduced in Sprint
6D.5B.

## Capture And Processing Layers

The evidence system is layered:

1. **Runtime capture**: core adapters, chambers, and cells observe controlled
   transitions and create minimal immutable records.
2. **Local custody**: the cell accepts bounded records before consequential
   success is committed.
3. **Evidence meta processing**: an environment meta slice consumes custody
   references, validates policy, aggregates, redacts, and routes records using
   Cadenza primitives.
4. **Durable ledger**: a persistence adapter appends authoritative records and
   supports idempotent replay.
5. **Projections**: relational state, realtime subscriptions, metrics, and UI
   views derive from the ledger and can be rebuilt.

The runtime capture channel is substrate, not a Cadenza business signal. It is
permitted by the doctrine that runtime substrate exists to execute and protect
primitives safely.

Evidence processing after custody should use Cadenza primitives. This preserves
the principle that Cadenza feature extensions use Cadenza while avoiding a
capture dependency on the system being observed.

## Evidence Profiles

Evidence profile is governed runtime policy. It is not an authored business
context field.

### Structural

Records mandatory execution lifecycle, causal relationships, authority
references, normalized outcomes, and context commitments. Raw business values
remain excluded.

Default for:

- business execution.
- ordinary meta-support execution.
- trusted-control execution, in addition to its stricter authority evidence.

### Boundary

Records acceptance, completion, failure, counts, commitments, and gap state at
the boundary of a support process without recording every internal task.

Default for the evidence-processing meta slice itself and for explicitly
approved high-volume support slices whose internal detail does not carry
independent business consequence.

### Debug

Adds bounded internal execution and language-native diagnostics under explicit
authority, expiry, retention, and access policy. It does not relax secret,
credential, or base-envelope prohibitions.

No `none` profile is permitted for affect-bearing business, meta-support, or
trusted-control execution. A support slice may use `boundary` only when its
internal actions are genuinely summarized by the evidenced boundary outcome.

## Recursive Meta Evidence

The legacy `sub_meta` mechanism solved a real recursion problem but should not
return as a hidden primitive class or signal-name convention.

V0 instead requires:

- records created while processing evidence are marked as
  `evidence_processing_internal` by trusted runtime metadata, not business
  context.
- internal processing uses the `boundary` profile by default.
- its mandatory boundary records append directly to local custody.
- those records have `processing_eligible: false` for the same processing
  pipeline, so they are persisted or projected without being re-enqueued into
  themselves.
- batch acceptance, completion, failure, record count, digest, and detected
  sequence gaps remain visible in production.
- debug authority may expose bounded internal task evidence through a distinct
  processing route.

This terminates recursion by explicit processing eligibility and trust-boundary
policy, not by making meta consequence invisible.

## Ordering, Delivery, And Projection

- source sequence and digest linkage establish local order.
- causal keys establish cross-source relationships.
- timestamps support operation and diagnosis only.
- downstream delivery is at least once; consumers deduplicate by
  `evidence_key` and require equal digest for replay.
- the same key with a different digest is an integrity failure.
- missing source sequences remain visible as gaps until repaired or explicitly
  declared unrecoverable.
- dependent records may arrive before their causes and must remain pending or
  partially projected without rewriting either record.
- mutable execution-status rows are projections, never evidence authority.
- rebuilding projections from the authoritative append history is a required
  capability.

## Relationship To Existing Evidence

This protocol unifies interpretation without flattening ownership:

- authority-operation evidence remains atomic with the authority mutation and
  is linked by policy-decision and evidence references.
- chamber materialization, activation, capability, and lifecycle evidence
  remains chamber-owned and links to applicable graph or task execution.
- cell lifecycle and containment evidence remains cell-owned and links to the
  capture-source identity.
- existing peer transport evidence maps to transport-attempt phases and gains
  explicit distribution and trace linkage.
- graph-conclusion evidence uses terminal provenance, relationship contribution
  policy, and context-composition outcomes from the graph-conclusion contract.

The Sprint 6D.4 substrate review replaced generic correlation with explicit
trace, source graph, source effect, optional signal or inquiry, distribution,
and transport-attempt identities. Caller idempotency remains separately named
and has no execution-identity meaning.

## Realtime Interpretation

Realtime means a consumer can observe evidence after local custody and before
durable global projection completes. It does not mean consumers observe an
uncommitted event or receive raw runtime context.

Realtime consumers receive ordered source records or projection deltas with:

- evidence and execution identities.
- custody status.
- known causal links.
- sequence-gap status.
- normalized lifecycle and outcome.
- authorized commitments and diagnostics.

Consumers must be able to distinguish authoritative evidence from a delayed,
partial, stale, or rebuilding projection.

## Conformance Scenarios

Implementation cannot close until shared fixtures and tests prove:

1. a linear local graph produces one trace, one graph execution, ordered task
   evidence, and one graph conclusion.
2. a split graph records relationship resolution, terminal provenance, and a
   deterministic composition outcome.
3. a detached signal creates a new graph execution with the same trace and no
   response obligation.
4. a local inquiry preserves one inquiry identity across requester and all
   responder graphs while assigning each responder a distinct responder
   identity.
5. a distributed delegation preserves trace and distribution identity across
   source, transport, target graph, and returned conclusion.
6. a distributed detached signal preserves trace identity without inventing a
   result relationship.
7. two transport attempts for one logical operation share a distribution key
   and have distinct attempt keys.
8. out-of-order records converge into the same projection as ordered records.
9. duplicate equal records are idempotent and duplicate conflicting records
   fail integrity validation.
10. custody failure prevents affect-bearing success.
11. terminal custody failure after execution start produces an indeterminate
    evidence-integrity failure, prohibits safe retry, and quarantines the
    capture source.
12. downstream projection failure after custody does not erase accepted
    execution evidence.
13. ordinary production evidence contains no raw business context or secrets.
14. evidence meta processing leaves production boundary evidence without
    recursive re-enqueue.
15. debug policy expands diagnostics without weakening containment or base
    evidence redaction.
16. sequence gaps and unrecoverable gaps remain explicit to realtime consumers.
17. authored context cannot inject or replace any authoritative execution
    identity.
18. exported evidence verifies against its cell-generation custody attestation.

## Non-Goals

- choosing the durable database or journal implementation.
- defining final relational tables or JSONB columns.
- returning graph results from the evidence system.
- treating logs, metrics, or tracing-vendor spans as evidence authority.
- defining automatic retry or exactly-once business execution.
- defining global trace quiescence or closure in V0.
- implementing concurrent chamber execution, which remains deferred.
- adding execution-evidence concerns to authored business contexts.
- implementing memory, UI, CLI, or agent-facing history features.
- preserving legacy schemas, APIs, names, or backward compatibility.

## Assumptions

- V0 may add execution identities to official runtime envelopes because the new
  major version has no backward-compatibility requirement.
- current serialized chamber execution simplifies local sequencing but the
  contract must remain valid after concurrency is introduced.
- one evidence-processing meta slice per active cell is sufficient for the
  first environment; automatic reconciliation and failover are later designs.
- the shared wire representation remains canonical JSON at this boundary unless
  a later approved contract proves another representation preserves
  cross-language coherence better.

# Stem Recovery And Fencing Closure

Date: 2026-07-19

## Context

Sprint 7F implemented and validated atomic PostgreSQL stem succession,
predecessor fencing, purpose-separated Cell-host recovery authority, ordinary
successor convergence, authority-mount continuity, and immutable takeover
evidence. Its database, hostile-boundary, ordinary Linux, and definitive
Linux/gVisor proofs passed, followed by a recursive closure review.

## Decision

Sprint 7F is accepted and closed following explicit user approval on
2026-07-19. Its execution plan moves to `completed`. Sprint 7G becomes the next
design gate and must prove the combined scale, supply, recovery, evidence, and
cleanup lifecycle before Sprint 7 as a whole can close.

## Consequences

- Migration 011 and the finalized Sprint 7F contracts are now historical
  authority and their checksums will not be rewritten.
- Later database repairs require a new ordered migration.
- Complete trusted-control extinction remains explicit operator/bootstrap
  restoration and is not silently moved into the recovery resolver.
- Sprint 7G may compose and pressure-test the closed capabilities but may not
  add unrelated features without a new design amendment.

## Alternatives

- Keep Sprint 7F open until Sprint 7G: rejected because its approved recovery
  boundary has independent implementation and closure evidence.
- Treat Sprint 7F closure as full Sprint 7 closure: rejected because supply and
  succession have not yet been proved together in one system lifecycle.
- Rewrite migration 011 during Sprint 7G repairs: rejected because closure
  establishes temporal stewardship of deployed migration history.
